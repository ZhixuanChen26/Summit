# Summit

A 2D side-scrolling survival platformer built in Unity. After an avalanche sweeps a vacationer off a mountain resort, he crashes into a lake with his feet frozen solid in ice. Unable to walk, his only option is to climb to the summit using an ice pickaxe and signal for rescue.

What starts as a desperate climb gradually reveals something far more dangerous beneath the snow.

---

## Story

A man arrives at an isolated mountain resort for vacation. While exploring outside, an avalanche sends him tumbling down the mountain into a frozen lake. With his feet encased in ice, he must scale the mountain one-handed using only an ice pickaxe.

As he climbs, the environment tells its own story — scattered gear from climbers who never made it back, crumbling old lodges, and subtle tremors underfoot. The mountain isn't just cold. It's alive.

**Plot twist:** The avalanche was caused by a volcano on the verge of eruption. The final act becomes a race down the mountain to outrun the lava.

---

## Mechanics

**Frozen feet movement** — The player cannot walk normally. All traversal relies on the ice pickaxe, reinforcing the character's physical limitations throughout.

**Breakable platforms** — Checkered ice tiles crumble sequentially when stood on, detected via collision script.

**Narrative audio triggers** — Unity Trigger Colliders at key story beats activate AI-voiced monologues. The first trigger, placed near a pair of lost skis buried in snow, plays a message of fear and despair to set the emotional tone.

**Volcano escape** — Reaching the summit triggers a final downhill chase as lava erupts beneath the player.

---

## Environmental Storytelling

The game uses no cutscenes. Story is communicated entirely through the world:

- Skulls and ski poles buried in snow — past climbers died here
- Broken lodges and abandoned gear — the mountain has a history
- Warm yellow/green lighting — safe zones where players can rest
- Cold blue tint — danger ahead
- Lava tubes beneath snowy caverns — the volcano is waking up
